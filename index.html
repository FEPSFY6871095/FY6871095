Abstract

Martian exploration requires propulsion system which can overcome the problems that arise from long distance travel and extended missions over years. Nuclear propulsion particularly nuclear thermal propulsion (NTP) and nuclear electric propulsion (NEP), offer highly efficient solution by reducing transit times by being able to carry less fuel onboard. This is important for human mental and physical well-being. This article assesses the principles of this technology, their advantages and whether they are mature enough to be deployed to a manned Mars mission today. It also considers the trade-off between astronaut safety and faster transit times whilst conveying propulsion design and political constraints. Ultimately the article highlights how nuclear propulsion will be pivotal in a human mission to Mars.
